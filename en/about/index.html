Web development since 1998.

Art school, then games QA, then agency frontend, now self-employed. In the web since 1998, self-employed since 2010. Not the shortest path, but I learned things along the way you don't learn in a bootcamp.

Who I am

I think in layouts, not algorithms — art school instead of computer science. After that, three years of quality assurance in the games industry: Goodgame Studios, Deep Silver FISHLABS, InnoGames. That's where you learn to find bugs before anyone else does.

Then YOOtheme — I worked at the framework maker. Then agency frontend at Tangram. Then self-employed. Every station several years, none of them as an intern.

How I work

One person, everything through one head. I use AI tools for code and tests. What AI doesn't handle: concept, design, the final call on quality.

Annemarie is an editor and translator (annemarie-andersen.de). We work together — she reviews everything before it goes live. Sounds obvious. It usually isn't.

Why not an agency?

I've worked at an agency myself for years. I know how agencies work: you call, explain your project to the sales person. They pass it to the project manager. Who briefs the designer. Who hands it to the developer. Three points where information gets lost.

With me, you talk to the person who writes the code. And that person has known your project since the first call.
