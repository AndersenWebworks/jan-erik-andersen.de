BFSG Compliance & Accessibility

Since June 2025, B2C websites in Germany must be accessible. Fines up to EUR 100,000. I build WCAG 2.1 AA compliant – as an update for existing sites or from scratch.

01

The Problem

The BFSG (Accessibility Strengthening Act) implements the European Accessibility Act into German law. Since June 2025: websites selling products or services to consumers must be accessible. Not as a recommendation, but as a legal requirement. Fines up to EUR 100,000.

Most websites don't meet the requirements. Missing contrast, no keyboard navigation, images without text alternatives, forms without labels – these aren't minor issues, they're violations. And overlay solutions like "AccessiWay" or "UserWay" aren't real accessibility – they're a band-aid on an open wound.

02

The Solution

Real accessibility is not a plugin you install. It's a building method – from the first line of code to the last piece of content.

  • WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance: The legally required standard. Contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility, text alternatives.
  • Semantic HTML: Correct heading hierarchy, ARIA labels, landmarks – so assistive technologies know where they are.
  • Forms and Interaction: Every form field with a label, every button with a description, every error message understandable.
  • Media: Images with alt text, videos with subtitles (where needed), no information conveyed only through color.
  • Testing: Not just automated tools (they find 30% of issues at most), but manual testing with screen reader and keyboard.

FAQ – BFSG & Accessibility

What is the BFSG?+

The BFSG (Accessibility Strengthening Act) implements the European Accessibility Act into German law. Since June 2025, websites selling products or services to consumers must be accessible according to WCAG 2.1 AA. Fines up to EUR 100,000.

Does the BFSG apply to my website?+

The BFSG applies to B2C websites selling products or services to end consumers. Pure B2B websites without consumer contact are currently exempt. When in doubt: build accessible anyway – it never hurts and interpretation is getting stricter.

What does WCAG 2.1 AA mean?+

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define technical standards for web accessibility. Level AA is the legally required standard. This includes: sufficient contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility, text alternatives for images.

Can my existing website be made accessible?+

In most cases, yes. Whether an update or a rebuild makes more sense depends on the technical condition of the existing site. Modern WordPress installations often just need a targeted update. With outdated technology, a rebuild is more cost-effective.

What does an accessible website cost?+
